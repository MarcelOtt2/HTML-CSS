            <h3>Her Remarkable Life and Achievements</h3>
            <p>Marie Skłodowska Curie (1867–1934) was a Polish and naturalized-French physicist and chemist who conducted pioneering research on radioactivity. She was the first woman to win a Nobel Prize, the first person and the only woman to win the Nobel Prize twice, and the only person to win the Nobel Prize in two scientific fields.</p>
            
            <p>Born in Warsaw, Poland, she moved to Paris in 1891 to study at the Sorbonne, where she met her future husband and research partner, Pierre Curie. Together, they discovered the elements polonium (named after her homeland) and radium.</p>
            
            <p>Her achievements include:</p>
            <ul>
                <li>Development of the theory of radioactivity</li>
                <li>Techniques for isolating radioactive isotopes</li>
                <li>Discovery of two elements: polonium and radium</li>
                <li>First woman to become a professor at the University of Paris</li>
                <li>Nobel Prize in Physics (1903) and Nobel Prize in Chemistry (1911)</li>
            </ul>
            
            <p>During World War I, Curie developed mobile radiography units to provide X-ray services to field hospitals. Her work with radioactive materials ultimately led to her death from aplastic anemia in 1934, but her legacy continues to inspire scientists around the world.</p>
